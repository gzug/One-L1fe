# Future Role of Notion as the Anchor Point for Data Entry, Calculation, and Visualization

Notion remains an important part of the system, but its role changes over time.

The goal is not to discard Notion immediately.
The goal is to use it where it is strongest right now, while gradually moving more complex logic into systems that are better suited for structured computation, automation, and long-term scalability.

This page explains that transition clearly, including the parts of the system that may not be visible inside Notion itself.

## Short Term

Notion is:

- the database
- the data-entry interface
- the dashboard
- the review surface

This means the user can continue to document, track, and review health data in a familiar environment, much like in the previous MVP.

In this phase, Notion acts as the practical working system for private use.
It should already be improved compared with the old version, with cleaner structure, clearer fields, and selected optimizations or lightweight automations where useful.

### Purpose of the short-term setup

- keep the system immediately usable
- avoid interrupting ongoing health documentation
- preserve the practical strengths of the old MVP
- improve clarity, structure, and maintainability
- create a better operational version without waiting for the full product

## Mid Term

Notion is still:

- the main entry point for manual input
- the place where results are displayed
- a review and organization layer

But more complex calculations gradually move outside Notion.

This is necessary because certain kinds of logic become difficult or fragile when they are handled only through Notion formulas and relations, especially when the system grows.

Examples of what may move outside Notion over time:

- more advanced calculations
- deeper rule-based analysis of raw data
- unit normalization and interpretation logic
- more reliable automation for data upload and assignment
- richer cross-metric analysis and context-aware interpretation

### Purpose of the mid-term setup

- reduce manual entry where possible
- support cleaner automation of raw-data ingestion
- model more complex relationships between data points
- improve analytical depth and reliability
- keep Notion useful without forcing it to carry the full logic burden

In this phase, some important processing may happen in the background and may not be directly visible as calculations inside Notion itself.
Notion still shows the results, but it is no longer the only place where those results are produced.

## Long Term

Notion becomes primarily:

- a review layer
- a dashboard layer
- an operations layer, if still desired

It is no longer the core engine.

At that stage, the main logic, analysis, and system behavior live elsewhere, for example in a dedicated app, backend, or web interface.

Notion may still remain useful for:

- reviewing outputs
- operational oversight
- structured summaries
- manual workflows
- personal knowledge organization

But it is optional at that point, not foundational.

## Final Direction

In the final state, Notion is expected to be fully replaced by:

- a dedicated app, or
- earlier, an intermediate web app / web interface

That system becomes the primary place for:

- data entry
- automation
- calculations
- analysis
- result presentation
- long-term user experience

## Summary

### Short term
Notion is the full working environment.

### Mid term
Notion stays visible and useful, but part of the logic moves outside it.

### Long term
Notion becomes optional and is no longer the main engine.

### Final
A dedicated app or web interface replaces it completely.
